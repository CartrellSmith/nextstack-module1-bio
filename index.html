My Story

Cartrell D. Smith is a dedicated learner and emerging developer whose journey has been shaped by resilience, purpose, and a commitment to growth. After facing health challenges that pushed him to rethink his career path, he made the bold decision to join NeXTStack, stepping into the world of software development with determination and clarity.

He brings with him a rich background of service, leadership, and communication. Before transitioning into tech, Cartrell worked as a Remote Customer Service Representative, an Elementary School Teacher, and a currently still a Youth Pastor. These roles strengthened his ability to guide, support, and connect with people from all walks of life. His time at NeXTStack as a Jr. Software Developer reflects his drive to build new skills while applying the professionalism he has carried throughout his career.

As he grows in the tech field, Cartrell is steadily building foundational abilities such as HTML, strong professional habits, and effective problem‑solving. These skills complement his long-standing strengths in customer service management, team leadership, public speaking, critical thinking, and project management across ministry and classroom environments.

Cartrell’s educational journey includes studying Software Development at the Mississippi Coding Academy, earning a B.A. in General Studies from Alcorn State University, completing an Associate of Arts at Copiah‑Lincoln Community College, and graduating from Natchez High School. Along the way, he also earned certifications as a Licensed Minister and through the Alternative Pathway to Teacher Licensing program.

His dedication and impact have not gone unnoticed. Cartrell has been honored with the Key to the City of Natchez, recognized with the Amazing Teacher Award, and celebrated through Staff Spotlight. These acknowledgments reflects both his character and his commitment to serving others.
